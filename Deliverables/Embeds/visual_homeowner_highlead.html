Different strategies may be needed to support varying ownership types in safely rebuilding their properties. We examined the percentage of significantly damaged properties in high lead grids by ownership type. Across owner types, over one in three significantly damaged properties are in high lead grids. More than half of the significantly damaged properties that are owned as rentals (renter-occupied) are in high lead grids. In West Altadena, 57% of renter-occupied, significantly damaged properties are in high lead areas and in East Altadena, 54% are.

West Altadena has a higher share of significantly damaged, owner-occupied properties in high lead areas. Nearly half (49%) of significantly damaged, owner-occupied properties in West Altadena are in high lead areas compared to 37% in East Altadena. Homeowners with significantly damaged properties or that are in high lead areas may require support in safely rebuilding their homes and mitigating hazards on their property.

The high rate of renter-occupied properties in high lead grids also necessitates an approach to ensuring landlords are taking measures to make rental properties safe for tenants. This includes renter-occupied properties that were left intact after the fire. In Altadena, 69% of renter-occupied properties that sustained no damage are in high lead grids. Additional soil testing at these properties and professional assessments for mitigation can help ensure properties are safe for homeowners and tenants to return to.
