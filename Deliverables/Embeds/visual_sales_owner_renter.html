We also examined the share of each ownership type that has sold since the Eaton Fire. Understanding which properties are selling at faster rates can help signal trends toward displacement and disaster capitalism, or corporate investors exploiting survivors during their recovery.1 As of September 2025, most properties that have sold since the fire were owner-occupied before fire, but these properties are not selling at a faster rate than other properties in Altadena. Owner-occupied properties in East and West Altadena saw the greatest number of sales after the Eaton Fire, but overall only two percent of owner-occupied properties have sold. A higher share of properties that were owned by a corporation before the fire have sold in East Altadena–8 percent of corporate-owned properties from January 2025 have sold.

Recent research from SAJE has shown how corporate investors have already exploited the Altadena community. Between February and July 2025, nearly half of parcels (47%) were purchased by corporate entities compared to only 10% bought by corporate entities in the prior year.2 Long-term strategies to support property owners throughout their recovery are needed to help preserve ownership in Altadena and reduce the track of speculation.
